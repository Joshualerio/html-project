Fresh Buko

Fresh buko, or young coconut, is a cherished dessert in Filipino cuisine due to its natural sweetness, versatility, and nutritional benefits. Its refreshing flavor makes it enjoyable on its own or as an ingredient in popular desserts like halo-halo. Beyond taste, fresh buko is hydrating and rich in electrolytes, contributing to its appeal as a healthier dessert option. Culturally, it evokes nostalgia and connection to Filipino traditions, enhancing its popularity. With its unique chewy texture and juicy water, fresh buko stands out as a delightful treat that resonates with many.

  • Ingredients

  • 2-3 young coconuts (meat and water)
  • 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk (optional)
  • 1 cup all-purpose cream (or whipped cream)
  • 1 cup fruit cocktail (canned or fresh, optional)
  • Additional diced fruits (mangoes, pineapples, strawberries, optional)
  • Â¼ cup chopped nuts (like cashews or almonds, optional)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, to taste)
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, cherries

    • Procedure

    • Open the coconuts, drain the water, and scoop out the meat.
    • In a bowl, combine the buko meat and water.
    • Stir in condensed milk and cream until well mixed.
    • Fold in fruit cocktail and additional fruits.
    • Add chopped nuts.
    • Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
    • Serve in bowls, garnished with cheese or cherries if desired.

    Enjoy your refreshing fresh buko dessert!
